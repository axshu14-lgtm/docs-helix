Before bidding opens on every single auction slot, members in the temporary server vote on whether the Pokémon is worth auctioning. This is the **Community Voting system** — one of the most distinctive features of Helixia auctions, and one that every participant should understand before joining.

---

## Why Does This System Exist?

A common problem in open Pokémon auctions is that sellers register Pokémon that are genuinely not worth the community's time — low IV, common species, or otherwise undesirable Pokémon that nobody wants to bid on anyway. When these slots run, everyone sits through 60+ seconds of silence waiting for bids that never come.

Helixia solved this by giving the community a voice. Before each slot goes to auction, **members vote on whether it deserves to be there**. If the majority says no, the slot is skipped and the session moves faster for everyone.

---

## How the Vote Works

After a seller posts their live Pokétwo info embed in the bid channel, the vote phase begins automatically.

<Steps>
  <Step title="The vote embed is posted">
    The bot posts a voting message in the 💰〢auction-bid channel tagging everyone present. The embed shows the Pokémon's info and explains the vote.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Members react with their vote">
    Two reaction options are added to the message:
    - ✅ — **Yes**, proceed with the auction
    - ❌ — **No**, skip this slot

    Members click the reaction that reflects their honest opinion of the Pokémon.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The vote closes after 15 seconds">
    The window is exactly **15 seconds**. After that, the bot tallies the reactions and determines the outcome.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Result is announced">
    If 60% or more of total votes are No, the slot is **skipped**. Otherwise, bidding opens normally.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## The Skip Threshold

A slot is skipped when **60% or more** of all votes cast are No votes.

| Vote outcome | Result |
|---|---|
| Less than 60% No | Bidding proceeds normally |
| 60% or more No | Slot is skipped |

If no one votes at all, the auction proceeds — the skip only triggers when there is an active majority against it.

---

## What Happens When a Slot Is Skipped

If your Pokémon gets voted out:

1. A skip embed is posted in the bid channel stating the Pokémon was skipped by community vote and showing the No vote percentage
2. You receive a **detailed DM** from the bot with:
   - The full vote breakdown (Yes votes, No votes, total voters)
   - A **named list of everyone who voted Yes and everyone who voted No**
   - Information on how to dispute the skip if you believe it was unfair
3. The channel is cleared and the auction moves immediately to the next slot

<Note>
  The DM you receive after a skip contains the complete voter list — you can see exactly who voted which way. This is intentional and transparent. The same information is also logged for staff review.
</Note>

---

## Votes Are Supervised — Vote Honestly

Every vote cast is **fully logged** and visible to staff. The log records:

- The Pokémon that was voted on
- Every member who voted Yes and every member who voted No
- The final result and percentages

<Warning>
  Members who are found to be **abusing the voting system** — for example, consistently voting No on perfectly good Pokémon out of spite, collusion, or to sabotage specific sellers — will face **strict action** from staff. This includes auction bans and server consequences.

  Please vote honestly based on whether the Pokémon genuinely deserves to be auctioned. Your vote reflects on you.
</Warning>

## Disputing a Skip

If your slot was skipped and you believe the community voted unfairly — for example, if your Pokémon had strong IVs and a fair starting bid but was still voted out — you have the right to dispute it.

**How to dispute:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review your DM">
    The bot DM you receive after a skip includes the full voter list. Review it to understand who voted No and how many people participated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open a support ticket in the main Helixia server">
    Go to the main Helixia server and open a support ticket. Explain that your auction slot was skipped and that you want it reviewed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Staff will review the vote logs">
    Staff have access to the complete vote logs. They will check whether the skip was legitimate or whether the voting system was misused. If the skip was unwarranted, appropriate action will be taken.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  When opening a dispute ticket, mention the Pokémon name, the session it was in (6 PM, 9 PM, or 11:30 PM), and the approximate date. This helps staff locate the correct vote log quickly.
</Tip>

## What Makes a Pokémon Vote-Safe?

While there is no guaranteed formula, here are the characteristics that tend to result in a Yes vote from the community:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="High IV percentage">
    This is the single biggest factor. Pokémon with 80%+ IVs are almost always welcomed into bidding. Pokémon with 50% or lower IVs — especially common species — are at high risk of being voted out.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Desirable species">
    Legendaries, pseudo-legendaries, and popular competitive or collector Pokémon attract Yes votes even at moderate IV percentages. A Dragonite with 70% IVs will generally do better than a Rattata with 90% IVs.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Fair starting bid">
    A starting bid that's aligned with the Pokémon's actual auction value signals that the seller is serious. Bringing a mediocre Pokémon with an inflated SB is a reliable way to get voted out.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Shiny Pokémon">
    Shiny variants almost always pass the vote regardless of IVs, because of their rarity and collector appeal.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## As a Voter — Your Responsibilities

If you participate in the vote (which you should), keep these principles in mind:

- **Vote based on the Pokémon's quality**, not on who the seller is
- **Do not coordinate votes** with others outside of your genuine opinion
- **Do not vote No on every slot** without consideration — this dilutes the purpose of the system
- Remember that the seller has met all requirements and made the effort to join the auction server — respect that by giving their Pokémon a fair evaluation

The community vote is a privilege, not a weapon. It exists to protect the quality of Helixia auctions for everyone.
