Bidding in a Helixia auction is fast, competitive, and real-time. This guide covers every rule and mechanic you need to know so you can participate confidently and avoid common mistakes that get bids rejected.

---

## Requirements to Bid

The same eligibility rules that apply to sellers also apply to bidders:

- **Level 5 or above** in the main Helixia server. The bot checks this on every bid. If you don't have the Level 5 role, your bid message will be silently rejected.
- **Member of the main Helixia server.** Checked automatically even when bidding from the temporary server.
- **Not blacklisted.** Blacklisted users cannot join the temporary server at all.

---

## How to Place a Bid

When a Pokémon's bidding phase is open, the 💰〢auction-bid channel unlocks and you can place bids by **simply typing the amount**. Nothing else — just the number.

Valid formats:

| What you type | What it means |
|---|---|
| `10000` | 10,000 pc |
| `10,000` | 10,000 pc |
| `10k` | 10,000 pc |
| `1m` | 1,000,000 pc |
| `2.5m` | Not valid — use `2500k` or `2500000` |

<Warning>
  Your message must contain **only** the bid amount — nothing else. Bids like `10k please`, `10k pc`, or `10k!` will be rejected because of the extra text. Type only the number, with an optional `k` or `m` suffix.
</Warning>

## The Starting Bid (SB)

Every Pokémon enters bidding with a **Starting Bid** set by the seller. Your first bid must be **at or above** the starting bid. The bot will silently ignore any bid below it.

The SB is displayed clearly in the bidding embed at the top of each slot. Make sure you see the SB before placing your first bid.

---

## Bid Increment Rules

The minimum amount your bid must exceed the current highest bid by depends on where the current highest bid sits. This is a **dynamic increment system**:

| Current Highest Bid | Minimum Increment |
|---|---|
| Below 1,000,000 pc | **1,000 pc** |
| 1,000,000 pc — 4,999,999 pc | **50,000 pc** |
| 5,000,000 pc and above | **100,000 pc** |

The bot will show the **minimum next bid** after every accepted bid so you always know exactly what you need to place.

**Example:** If the current highest bid is 800,000 pc, the minimum next bid is 801,000 pc. If the bid rises to 1,200,000 pc, the minimum next bid becomes 1,250,000 pc.

<Note>
  You are always free to bid higher than the minimum increment. There is no rule against jumping the bid by a large amount — many competitive bidders do this intentionally to deter others.
</Note>

## High Bid Confirmation (Bids of 1M pc or More)

For bids of **1,000,000 pc or more**, the bot will ask you to **confirm your bid** before it is accepted. A confirmation message will appear with a **Yes** and **No** button.

- You have **30 seconds** to click Yes to confirm
- If you don't confirm in time, the bid is automatically cancelled
- If someone else places a higher bid while you're confirming, your bid is rejected even if you click Yes

This system exists to prevent accidental misbids at high amounts. **Misbids will not be honored** — always double-check your amount before confirming.

<Warning>
  If you place a high bid and then click Yes to confirm, you are committing to that amount. Do not bid amounts you are not prepared to pay. Refusing to complete a trade after winning is a serious violation.
</Warning>

## The Bidding Timer

Each slot has a **60-second countdown**. The timer starts when bidding opens and **resets to 60 seconds with every valid new bid**. This means:

- Low-interest Pokémon may sell in as little as 60 seconds
- Highly competitive Pokémon can run for many minutes if bids keep coming in
- A **"Final Seconds"** warning is posted when ~5 seconds remain with no new bids
- A **10-second reminder** is also periodically sent showing the current highest bid and minimum next bid

When the 60 seconds fully expire with no new bid, the auction closes and the highest bidder wins.

## Bidding Rules & Restrictions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="You cannot outbid yourself">
    If you are currently the highest bidder, you cannot place another bid. You must wait for someone else to outbid you before you can bid again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The seller cannot bid on their own Pokémon">
    The owner of the Pokémon being auctioned is blocked from placing bids on their own slot. This is enforced automatically.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Maximum bid cap">
    No bid may exceed **30,000,000 pc (30M)**. Any bid above this amount is automatically rejected.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Bids must be the entire message">
    Your bid message must contain only the amount — no extra words, punctuation, or text. Anything other than a plain number (with optional k/m) will be ignored.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Malformed numbers are rejected">
    Formats like `37,1k` (a comma followed by a single digit before a suffix) are rejected as malformed. Use clean numbers: `37k`, `37100`, `37,100`.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="You must be in the main Helixia server">
    If you are not a member of the main Helixia server, your bid will be rejected. The bot cross-checks your membership on every bid attempt.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## After the Bidding Closes

When the timer expires and you are the highest bidder:

1. The bot posts a **"Sold!"** embed naming you as the buyer with the final price
2. A **private trade thread** is automatically created between you and the seller in the bid channel
3. You will receive a **DM** confirming your win and directing you to the thread

The auction immediately moves on to the next slot while your trade thread runs separately in the background.

See the [Completing a Trade guide](/auctions/completing-a-trade) for exactly what to do inside the trade thread.

---

## What Happens if a Pokémon Gets No Bids?

If the 60-second timer expires without a single valid bid being placed, the Pokémon is marked as **Unsold**. The seller receives nothing, the slot is logged as unsold, and the auction moves to the next registered Pokémon.

This typically happens when:
- The starting bid is set too high relative to the Pokémon's perceived value
- The Pokémon is not desirable enough to attract any bidders
- The session has low attendance

## Tips for Winning

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Watch the minimum increment as the price rises">
    The increment jumps significantly at 1M and 5M. Factor this in when deciding whether to keep bidding — the price can escalate very quickly in those ranges.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use round numbers to signal intent">
    Bidding something like `1,500,000` instead of `1,050,000` when the minimum is `1,050,000` signals strong interest and can discourage other bidders from continuing.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Have your bid ready before the warning appears">
    The "Final Seconds" warning appears with only ~5 seconds left. If you plan to bid last-minute, have your message typed and ready to send the moment you see the warning — don't start typing after it appears.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Know your limit before you start">
    It's easy to get caught up in a bidding war and go over budget. Decide your maximum amount before bidding begins so you don't commit to more than you can trade.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
